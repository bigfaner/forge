# Example: Proposing Approaches

After understanding requirements, propose 2-3 **business approaches** conversationally.

**Forbidden**: Approaches must not involve specific technology selection (frameworks, languages, libraries, databases, architectural patterns, etc.). Describe "what to achieve" not "how to implement". However, non-functional constraints (e.g., performance requirements, platform requirements, security/compliance) are allowed.

## Structure

```
Based on our discussion, I see 3 approaches:

**Approach A (Recommended):** [Business-level description — what users can do, what changes in their workflow]
- Business value: ...
- Trade-offs: ...

**Approach B:** [Business-level description]
- Business value: ...
- Trade-offs: ...

**Approach C:** [Business-level description]
- Business value: ...
- Trade-offs: ...

I recommend Approach A because [reasons]. What do you think?
```

## Tips

- **Lead with your recommended option** and explain why
- **Include trade-offs** for each approach (business impact, user experience, scope)
- Let user make the final decision
- **Stay at business level** — describe functionality and user impact, not implementation
